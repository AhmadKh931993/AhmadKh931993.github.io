The Challenge

The Problem

Despite strong offline demand, the Escape Room website was failing to convert visitors into bookings. Three core issues were identified after initial analysis.

Low conversion rate from visit to booking, confusing and cluttered navigation structure, and an outdated UI that reduced user trust and credibility. Users were dropping off early in the journey despite clear intent to book.

Phase 01

Research

To validate and understand these issues, a mixed-method research approach was conducted combining analytics, user interviews, usability testing, and A/B testing.

Analytics

Quantitative data revealed key behavioral problems across the site:

  • High bounce rate across landing pages — visitors weren't engaging
  • Significant drop-off during the booking flow — intent wasn't converting
  • Poor mobile performance despite mobile being the primary traffic source

User Interviews & Usability Testing

Through interviews and task-based usability testing with 5 participants, three recurring pain points emerged:

  • Users struggled to locate the booking CTA
  • Pricing information was unclear and difficult to compare
  • The visual design felt outdated and untrustworthy

A/B Testing

A/B testing was conducted over a 4-week period to validate design improvements and measure performance impact against the original version. This ensured every design decision was backed by real behavioral data — not assumptions.

Phase 02

Design Decisions

Insights from research directly informed the redesign strategy. Each decision focused on reducing friction, improving clarity, and increasing user trust throughout the experience.

  • Simplified navigation — from a cluttered structure down to 5 clear top-level items
  • Visible booking CTA — redesigned homepage with a highly accessible call-to-action
  • Streamlined booking flow — reduced to 3 focused steps with clear progress indicators
  • Refreshed visual identity — dark, immersive design aligned with the brand's character
  • Mobile-first approach — prioritized the majority mobile user base throughout
Outcomes

Results

The A/B test over 4 weeks showed significant improvements across all measured metrics. Post-launch feedback confirmed that users found the experience clearer, faster, and more trustworthy.
